Design a luxury culinary website for Hearty Table, a fine dining concept from Nairobi, Kenya that reimagines Kenyan cuisine through a high-end, editorial lens. The brand is rooted in culture, storytelling, and intentionality — think Michelin-starred restraint meets African warmth.

VISUAL IDENTITY & DESIGN LANGUAGE

Color palette: Cream/off-white background (#F2EFE9), charcoal/near-black text (#2C2C2C), warm grey accents. No saturated colors — the palette should feel linen, stone, and ash.
Typography: Pair a classical high-contrast serif (similar to Cormorant Garamond or Didot) for all headings and display text with wide letter-spacing — think H E A R T Y   T A B L E tracked out generously. Body text in a clean, lightweight sans-serif at small size. All caps for navigation labels.
Aesthetic reference: thomaskeller.com/tfl — minimal, editorial, quiet luxury. Full-bleed photography, sparse text, no clutter. The negative space does the talking.
Decorative motif: Incorporate fine botanical line-art leaf illustrations (as seen in the Hearty logo) subtly in corners and section dividers — never overpowering, always understated.
Logo: Place the "H" with heart logomark (a serif letter H with a small heart on the crossbar, on a leaf illustration background) in the top-center of the navigation on a cream background.


SITE STRUCTURE & PAGES
Build the following pages:
1. Homepage

Fixed top navigation bar: cream background, logo centered, nav links in all-caps spaced serif on either side (ABOUT, PHILOSOPHY, THE EXPERIENCE, COLLABORATE, CONTACT)
Hero section: Full-bleed, edge-to-edge cinematic photograph of a beautifully plated Kenyan dish. Over the image, the brand name H E A R T Y   T A B L E in large tracked-out white serif. Beneath it, one line of italic copy: "Kenyan cuisine, elevated."
Thin horizontal rule below hero, then a single centered paragraph in small caps: "Nairobi · Kenya"
"Our Philosophy" teaser section: Two-column layout — left side is a large portrait photograph, right side has a headline like ROOTED. REFINED. INTENTIONAL. and 2–3 lines of body text pulled from the concept note. No button, just text.
"The Experience" section: Dark charcoal background (full-width breakout). Centered serif heading: GATHER in large tracked type. Below, italic subheading: "An intimate multi-course dinner for fifty." Small body text describing the curated dinner event. A subtle CTA text link: LEARN MORE →
Ingredient / sourcing strip: Horizontal scrolling or grid of 3 minimal tiles — each a close-up photograph of a Kenyan ingredient with a one-word label beneath in small caps (e.g., SUKUMA, ARROW ROOT, TILAPIA)
Collaboration section: Light background, centered text block about the 3ZERO Kenya partnership and the youth chef collaboration. Clean, editorial, no imagery.
Footer: Logo centered, minimal nav links in small caps, social icons as thin line icons, copyright line.

Full-bleed header image (kitchen or plating detail)

Long-form editorial body text from the concept note — about Hearty's identity, philosophy, and mission
Pull quote styled in large italic serif, centered, with thin rules above and below: "Kenyan ingredients and traditions are already luxurious — we just need to present them that way."

4. Philosophy Page

Minimal, text-driven page — like a manifesto
Sections for: Sustainability & Conscious Sourcing / Kitchen Culture / Culinary Identity
Each section has a small botanical leaf illustration as a section marker

5. Collaborate / Contact Page

Simple centered layout
Heading: LET'S BUILD SOMETHING
Short text about partnership and collaboration intent
A minimal contact form: Name, Email, Message — no borders, just thin underlines on input fields


UI COMPONENT RULES

Navigation: No hamburger on desktop. Links in letter-spacing: 0.2em, font-weight light, all caps, no underlines — hover state is a thin underline animation
Buttons/CTAs: No filled buttons. Use text links with → arrows or thin-bordered ghost buttons only
Dividers: Single-pixel hairline rules in warm grey
Photography style: Dark, moody, intimate — close-up plating shots, hands at work in kitchen, raw Kenyan ingredients. No bright food photography.
Spacing: Generous. Sections breathe. Padding top/bottom on each section minimum 120px.
Mobile: Stack all two-column layouts. Hero text scales down but stays tracked. Navigation collapses to a minimal drawer.


TONE
The site should feel like entering a private dining room — hushed, intentional, beautiful. It should not feel like a restaurant ordering platform. Every word and visual should communicate that this is an experience, not a transaction.
